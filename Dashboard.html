Risk Factors
3 PRIMARY · MATERIAL
01
FAA Grounding of New Glenn (Apr 2026)
NG-3 BE-3U upper-stage anomaly stranded AST SpaceMobile's BlueBird 7. Mishap investigation indefinite; combined with 2CAT facility damage, 2026 cadence target of 12 launches is materially impaired. Each missed quarter compresses the launch-segment EV by ~$3–5B and delays Blue Moon Mk1 "Endurance" certification.
High
02
TeraWave CapEx Burden
5,408-satellite LEO/MEO buildout (Q4 2027 onward) requires an estimated $30–45B in cumulative CapEx through 2031. Negative free cash flow window of 4+ years; success contingent on enterprise customer adoption against entrenched Starlink and parallel Amazon Leo. Q/V-band reliability remains commercially unproven at scale.
High
03
Cadence Scaling Risk
Path from ~3 NG launches in 2026 to 60/year by 2030 implies a 20x ramp — historically unprecedented outside SpaceX. Requires a second pad, BE-4 production scaling, and TeraWave manifest absorption. Each 25% miss vs. cadence target translates to ~$8–12B EV impairment on the launch segment.
Mid
04
Customer & Program Concentration
Top-3 backlog exposure: Amazon Leo, NASA Artemis (Blue Moon Mk2), and DoD NSSL Phase 3. Cancellation, descope, or NASA budget reauthorization risk could cascade across multiple segments simultaneously.
Mid
05
Suborbital Pause
Announced 2-year suspension of New Shepard tourism flights compresses suborbital segment NPV. Modest absolute impact (~$1B EV) but a sentiment headwind on the IPO roadshow.
Low
